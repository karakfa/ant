Self-Study Reading List

Analytic Number Theory

A basic-to-advanced reference list — textbooks, free lecture notes, and YouTube lectures — for a reader with a strong analysis background and no prior number theory.

T0
Optional warm-up
Elementary number theory refresher
An Introduction to the Theory of Numbers
Niven, Zuckerman & Montgomery · Wiley · 5th ed., 1991 · ISBN 978-0471625469

Congruences, quadratic reciprocity, primitive roots — the vocabulary Apostol assumes you already have. Skim the relevant chapters only if elementary number theory feels rusty.

WorldCat / library access no legitimate free copy — actively sold title
A Basic Course in Number Theory
Shripad Garge · NPTEL / IIT Bombay · 64 lectures, free video

Full, complete video course — rare for this material. Builds elementary number theory up toward the prime number theorem and quadratic reciprocity. Slower-paced than Apostol; good as reinforcement if you want lecture-style review of T0 material.

T1
Core text
Where almost all the work happens
Introduction to Analytic Number Theory
Tom M. Apostol · Springer UTM · 1976

The standard on-ramp. Self-contained, assumes strong analysis and no prior number theory. Covers elementary methods, arithmetic functions, Dirichlet series, characters, and full proofs of Dirichlet's theorem and the Prime Number Theorem. Expect to spend most of your study time here.

Borrow free — Internet Archive Springer publisher page
18.785 Lecture Notes (2015–16 vintage)
Andrew V. Sutherland · MIT · free notes

Companion notes, not a substitute. Lectures 15–16, "The Riemann zeta function and the Prime Number Theorem," are among the cleanest expositions of the Newman/Zagier contour-integral PNT proof anywhere — read alongside Apostol's own proof for a second angle on the hardest result in the book.

math.mit.edu/classes/18.785/2015fa course site reorganizes yearly — search "MIT 18.785 Sutherland notes" if link has moved
Analytic Number Theory — Oxford C3.8 (sample lectures)
James Maynard · University of Oxford · 2 of 16 lectures public

Fields Medallist Maynard's actual undergraduate course tracks Apostol's later chapters almost exactly — prereqs are "complex analysis + elementary number theory," goal is proving PNT via zeta zeros. Only two lectures are public; watch the first as a map of the whole subject before you start.

T2
Second pass
Same theorems, different angle
Multiplicative Number Theory
Harold Davenport, rev. Hugh L. Montgomery · Springer GTM 74 · 3rd ed., 2000

Shorter and denser than Apostol. Covers similar core ground but pushes further into the zero-free region and explicit formula. Re-proving theorems you already know, by a different route, is where real fluency starts.

Springer publisher page no legitimate free copy
The Prime Number Theorem
G.J.O. Jameson · LMS Student Texts 53 · Cambridge, 2003

Slim and focused entirely on PNT and its variants, including the elementary (Selberg–Erdős) proof and PNT for arithmetic progressions. Good if you want to dwell on this one result before moving on.

Cambridge Core — LMS Student Texts
246B, Notes 4 — The Riemann zeta function and the prime number theorem
Terence Tao · blog notes, free

The best single piece of writing I know for the "why does ζ have to be involved" intuition. Read whenever the PNT machinery starts feeling like a bag of tricks rather than a structure.

terrytao.wordpress.com
T3
Graduate breadth
Toward the research literature
Introduction to Analytic and Probabilistic Number Theory
Gérald Tenenbaum · AMS GSM 163 · 3rd ed., 2015

Broader and more modern than Apostol/Davenport — brings in probabilistic number theory, mean values of multiplicative functions, and sieve basics. A natural bridge before Montgomery & Vaughan.

AMS author page AMS Bookstore
Multiplicative Number Theory I: Classical Theory
Hugh L. Montgomery & Robert C. Vaughan · Cambridge Studies in Advanced Mathematics 97 · 2007

Explicitly written to prepare readers "for intelligent reading of the more advanced research literature." Assumes only a standard math degree but moves fast — Vinogradov-type zero-free regions, large sieve basics, deeper L-function theory. The natural next step after T1/T2.

Borrow free — Internet Archive Cambridge Core
Math 229 — Introduction to Analytic Number Theory, lecture notes
Noam Elkies · Harvard · free notes

Informal, opinionated companion notes at roughly this level — a useful cross-check when a Tenenbaum or Montgomery–Vaughan argument feels terse.

people.math.harvard.edu/~elkies/M229.15
T4
Pick a branch
Specialized directions
An Introduction to Sieve Methods and Their Applications
Alina Carmen Cojocaru & M. Ram Murty · Cambridge, LMS Student Texts 66

The gentler entry into sieve theory — Brun, Selberg — before tackling Greaves below.

Cambridge Core
Sieves in Number Theory
George Greaves · Springer, Ergebnisse der Mathematik

Comprehensive treatment once the basics from Cojocaru–Murty feel solid.

publisher: link.springer.com (search title)
The Hardy-Littlewood Method
R.C. Vaughan · Cambridge Tracts in Mathematics 125 · 2nd ed.

The circle method — Waring's problem, Goldbach. A genuinely different flavor of analytic technique from everything above.

Cambridge Core
The Theory of the Riemann Zeta-Function
E.C. Titchmarsh, rev. D.R. Heath-Brown · Oxford, 2nd ed.

The classical deep-RH reference — dense, exhaustive, the book everyone eventually opens for a precise statement about zeta's zeros.

no legitimate free copy
Analytic Number Theory
Henryk Iwaniec & Emmanuel Kowalski · AMS Colloquium Publications 53 · 2004

Encyclopedic — often treated as the field's reference text rather than read cover to cover. Many consider this the natural ceiling of a self-study path before specializing into research.

AMS Bookstore
Sieve methods — Distinguished Lecture Series talk
James Maynard · Bristol, 2024 · free video

A colloquium-style overview of what sieve methods are and what they can (and can't) say about primes. Good motivation-builder before committing to the sieves branch.

search "James Maynard Bristol Distinguished Lecture sieve methods 2024" — listing page, not a direct stable video URL
A note on the gaps. Several canonical texts here (Davenport, Niven–Zuckerman–Montgomery, Titchmarsh) are still actively sold and under copyright, so no legitimate free full-text version exists — publisher pages are linked instead of the pirated PDFs that turn up in general search. Apostol and Montgomery–Vaughan are both legally borrowable through Internet Archive's lending program, which is the real exception. For the rest, university or public library access — often free via interlibrary loan — is the legitimate route. Good full video courses for analytic number theory specifically are genuinely rare online; most of what's listed here is either a complete free course at a more elementary level, or a small set of sample/orientation lectures from the real thing.
Basic → advanced reading order: Apostol → Davenport → Tenenbaum → Montgomery & Vaughan → Iwaniec–Kowalski, branching at T4 Compiled June 2026
